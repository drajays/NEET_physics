Trigonometric Waveforms

The six trigonometric functions plotted against an angle x in radians, over −2π to 2π. Toggle any curve, and hover the graph to read exact values. Vertical dashed lines mark the asymptotes.

What each curve does

y = sin xStarts at 0, peaks at +1, troughs at −1. Period 2π.
y = cos xStarts at +1. Leads sine by π/2. Period 2π.
y = tan x= sin x / cos x. Period π. Asymptotes where cos x = 0.
y = cot x= cos x / sin x. Period π. Asymptotes where sin x = 0.
y = sec x= 1 / cos x. Period 2π. |sec x| ≥ 1. Asymptotes where cos x = 0.
y = csc x= 1 / sin x. Period 2π. |csc x| ≥ 1. Asymptotes where sin x = 0.

Basic Trigonometric Functions

x-axis in radians (ticks at multiples of π/2). y clamped to −3 … 3 so the unbounded curves (tan, cot, sec, csc) stay readable.

Exact values at standard angles

UD = undefined (the function has an asymptote there). 1/√2 = √2/2 ≈ 0.707, √3/2 ≈ 0.866, 1/√3 = √3/3 ≈ 0.577, 2/√3 ≈ 1.155.
