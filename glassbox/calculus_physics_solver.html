Q1 Position & Velocity Zero Time

Topic: Kinematics
The position x of a particle moving along the x-axis varies with time t as x = 3t² - 2t³, where x is in meters and t is in seconds. At what time will the velocity of the particle become zero?
Glass Box Hint
Velocity is the first derivative of position with respect to time (v = dx/dt). Differentiate the position expression, equate it to zero, and solve for t > 0.
