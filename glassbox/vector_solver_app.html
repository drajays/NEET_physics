Shortcut · the cosine controls the whole resultant

Because R = √(A² + B² + 2AB·cosθ), the resultant dances to cosθ — maximum (A + B) at 0°, Pythagorean √(A²+B²) at 90°, minimum |A − B| at 180°. For two equal vectors it collapses to the one-line cheat code R = 2A·cos(θ/2).

Why 120° is the magic angle: if two friends each pull a block with 100 N and their ropes are 120° apart, the net force is exactly 100 N (R = 2·100·cos60° = 100 N). Whenever you see a regular hexagon or vectors spreading evenly around a circle, hunt for 120° angles — you can often cancel them in your head. (Try this live in Q36 and Q37 of the question bank.)
